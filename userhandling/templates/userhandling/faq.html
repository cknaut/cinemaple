WHAT IS CINEMAPLE?

Toni Servillo in La Grande Bellezza (Paolo Sorrentino, 2013)

Cinemaple is an online-platform designed for organizing Movie Nights with your friends, share good times and a common passion for the art of cinema. It was developped as an open-source project, which means that its code is public and shared to whoever would like to use. However, don't worry, your data is protected (see section below).

Cinemaple attempts to provide a standalone solution to organize Movie Nights, while not involving social networks.

In particular, Cinemaple users can invite guests to their Movie Nights. Guests can let the Hosts know which crêpe toppings they will bring and vote for their favorite movie.

And the rest is history. We want you to have as much fun as Jep Gambardella in La Grande Belleza.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

Cinemaple is a userfriendly solution for organizing, sharing, and attending Movie Nights.

Users who want to organize their own Movie Nights are Hosts of a Location. There can be several Hosts per Location. Hosts can send invitation links to their friends, who then register as Guests of a Location. Hosts can also delegate invitation rights to Guests, which are called Ambassadors.

The Host of a Location can organize a Movie Night at his/her Location. A Movie Night consists in a theme, a short description, and a selection of movies. The Guests are invited to the Movie Night and get to vote for their favorite movie(s). Guests will be notified about upcoming movie nights per email and will be directed to the Current Movie Night page, where they can register for the movienight.

During the signup, the Guest can vote for the selected users, and indicate which toppings he/she brings for the crêpes.

Finally, a voting mechanism (see below) selects the winning movie, the Guests gather at the Host's location and share good times while watching the chosen movie, simple as that!

What's with the crêpes?

You know you want this

Traditionally, Cinemaple Movie Nights start out with a crêpes feast. While the Hosts are in charge of diligently preparing the crêpes, Guests are asked to provide toppings and indicate which topings they'll bring during the Movie Nights registration. These toppings can range from sweet classics like chocolate chips to salty delights like goat cheese.

How does the movie voting work?

We know that picking a movie that will please everyone can be tough as nails. We have thus outsourced this task to a robust, preference-ranking based voting mechanism. For each movie night, Guest are presented with a choice of movies. Upon registration, Guests will be asked to rate every movie, which will create a ranking of movies. Then, a preference-ranking based voting mechanism is employed to find the winning movie. This voting mechanism ensures that the winning movie would win every one-to-one vote against every other movie. This way we hope to extract a winner which maximizes consensus during the Movie Night. For the voting-method nerds out here, we employ the Schulze method, which is a Condorcet-type method and we provide access the the intermediate results of the voting mechanism for full transparency.

I WANT TO INVITE MY FRIENDS TO CINEMAPLE

Figure: inviting friends to Cinemaple.

For now, the only way to access Cinemaple is via an invitation link from another user.

There are two types of users that can invite other users on Cinemaple: Hosts and Ambassadors. Hosts are the main administrators of a physical location. Typically, they are the tenants of a place in real life. They can organize Movie Nights and invite people. They can also delegate their invitation power to Ambassadors, who can then invite Guests to the Host's location.

Other users can be linked to a location as Guests (which means they can be invited to Movie Nights at a given location) by receiving an activation link from a Host or Ambassador.

To see your status and associated location, see User Info.
